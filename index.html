Frequently Asked Questions

Common Conditions Treated in Pediatric Cardiac Care

+

Pediatric cardiologists manage a wide variety of congenital heart lesions including congenital heart defects especially atrial septal defect (ASD), ventricular septal defect (VSD), and tetralogy of fallot. They also manage congenital heart disease involvement and heart failure (systolic or diastolic or both). We also offer post-operative care and regular follow-up for valvular and congenital heart surgeries.

When Is The Best Time To Consult A Pediatric Cardiologist?

+

You should contact a paediatric cardiologist if symptoms such as regular chest pain, shortness of breath, severe tiredness with no reason, blackouts, or a heart murmur that has been identified by a paediatrician appear to be trouble for your child. If the child has a family history of any heart illnesses, it is essential to undergo screening at an early age for any possible problems as well.

What are the other areas Of interest within the Specialty Of Pediatric Cardiology??

+

Pediatric cardiology has various other subspecialties which include pediatric congenital heart disease, pediatric heart rhythm disturbances and electrophysiology and, pediatric heart failure and cardiomyopathies, catheter-based interventional procedures and pediatric cardiothoracic surgery.

What is 'minimally invasive surgery' in pediatric cardiac care, and when is it used?

+

Minimally invasive surgery for congenital heart defects creates smaller cuts on the body. This type of surgery can be done for heart valve repairs, closure of septal defects, certain types of congenital heart defects and so on. The intention is to decrease recovery time, reduce the amount of scarring, and lower the risk of infections compared to traditional procedures that might require open heart surgery.

What is the average recovery time associated with paediatric cardiac surgery? How long do the children have to take rest?

+

The recovery durations following surgery may differ according to the number of surgeries that the child has gone through and the other health factors of the child. There are children who may not take more than a few days or weeks to recover from subcutaneous surgeries but children undergoing major surgeries may take several months to recover and can return to normalcy only after gaining sufficient strength.

Are there risks involved in pediatric cardiac surgeries?

+

All procedures entail a risk, whether it is of contracting an infection, experiencing anesthesia complications, poor heart rhythms, the potential of bleeding, or suffering from blood clots. But, the risks are reduced as much as possible and so are the consequences as pediatric cardiac surgery has a specialized approach by the complete team of doctors.

How do I prepare my child for pediatric cardiac surgery?

+

The parent’s role is critical hence concerns regarding emotions should also be dealt with emotionally right before and after the operation. Respective of where the child is going under the knife, what would help the parent relax is how effortlessly the step takes, as it is a pre-softening phase promising a variety of tests that include blood tests, imaging studies, as well as much more in the procedure segment.

What should I be ready for on my first consultation at a pediatric cardiology clinic?

+

The pediatric cardiologist will evaluate medical history and chart a physical examination during consultation. The doctor will also potentially perform diagnostic tests such as a chest X-ray, an ECG, and other such tests. The relevant tests and other connotations for the ailment would be discussed by the doctor and further steps regarding treatment would be provided.

Can pediatric heart conditions be prevented?

+

As far as congenital heart defects are concerned, some congenital ones may have higher chances of causing adverse consequences but other than that, this is a fairly good point of view ensuring that particular amendments within, appropriate substance use practices along with proper use of maternal diabetes or any other infection control during the process of giving birth. For those kids that already have heart issues, regular follow ups and a healthy lifestyle would serve to reduce their risk.
